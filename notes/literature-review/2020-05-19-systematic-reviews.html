Source

# Propose a relevant question

# PICOS

  • Problem/Population: Example - Patellofemoral Pain
  • Interventions: Example - Patellar taping
  • Comparisons: Example - Control, Placebo, other Interventions
  • Outcomes: Example - Pain, function, biomechanics

The outcome is very important and will dictate the study design. For example, if your focus is to study biomechanics, then


PICO for biomechanics
P: Patellofemoral Pain
I: Not applicable
C: Controls or asymptomatic individuals
O: Gluteal muscle activity

# Study design

  • Keep broad until you know what is out gathered
  • Easiest to modify later
  • Doesn't change questions
  • Aim for highest level of evidence
  • Can also narrow using other PICO items

# Criteria for study design

  • Should not be ambiguous
  • Address components of PICO
  • Consider what should be excluded

# Design a search strategy

  • Keywords - Balance between sensitivity and specificity. What similar reviews exist to guide.
  • Databases - more than 3
  • Other search strategies
    • Manual
    • Reference lists
    • Search author names
    • Contact prominent authors
    • Search for citations of key papers
    • Google scholar
