# How to read

# Make a habit and have low target

Just one page a day will do, but do regularly.

# Read a book before getting on to internet

Internet is place full of distractions. Read before you get into that rabbit hole. Read first thing in the morning.

# Pair with other habits you already have - Habit bunching

This way, it is easier to pick up reading as a new habit. Listen to audio books.

# Identify yourself as a book lover

Don't say you are a book hater. By this way, you are subconsciously making it easier

# How to remember

# Pre-reading

Go over the TOC. Go to each chapter and read the abstract and ending. By this you are preparing your mind on what to expect.

# Take notes after you read

Read a section, and then take notes on the most important details.

# Find additional sources

Find other related text and explore how this was used.

# Memory

Move stuff from short term memory to long term memory. Don't overload information. Don't multitask. Don't get distracted.

# Recall

  • After you have read something, simply look away and visualise what you have just read. Repeat.

# The Feynman technique

  • Take something you want to understand
  • Write out and explanation as though you want to teach it to others
  • If you get stuck go back and re-learn
  • Simplify. Get rid of technical / convoluted language

To do this use Analogies, these compare the study that is in context to something that is well known.
